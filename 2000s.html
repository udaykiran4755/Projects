2000s: From PCs to Pockets

The new millennium. Social networks connected us, smartphones got smart, and the web became truly interactive.

The iPod (2001)

It wasn't the first MP3 player, but Apple's iconic design and seamless iTunes integration put "1,000 songs in your pocket" and changed music forever.

Wikipedia (2001)

A free, collaborative encyclopedia. It democratized knowledge and became a cornerstone of the internet, making information accessible to all.

Social Media (2004)

Facebook (then Thefacebook) launched, kicking off the era of social networking that would define the decade and change how we interact online and off.

YouTube (2005)

"Broadcast Yourself." The simple idea of a video-sharing platform exploded, changing media, entertainment, and a new generation of creators.

The iPhone (2007)

Apple's "Jesus phone" combined an iPod, a phone, and an internet communicator. It killed the physical keyboard and launched the app economy.
