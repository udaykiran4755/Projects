1990s: The Digital Frontier

The decade that brought the world online, put music in our pockets (sort of), and made 3D graphics a reality.

Key Innovations of the 90s

World Wide Web (1991)

World Wide Web (1991)

Tim Berners-Lee's invention that connected the world and made information universally accessible.

Linux (1991)

Linux (1991)

Linus Torvalds released the open-source kernel that would go on to power servers, Android, and more.

First GSM Phone (1992)

First GSM Phone (1992)

The Nokia 1011 was the first mass-produced GSM phone, featuring the new keypad layout and paving the way for text messaging.

The DVD (1995)

The DVD (1995)

The new optical disc standard that replaced VHS and brought high-quality digital video to homes.

Wi-Fi (1997)

Wi-Fi (1997)

The first version of the 802.11 protocol was released, allowing devices to connect to networks wirelessly.

Google Search (1998)

Google Search (1998)

A new search engine by Larry Page and Sergey Brin revolutionized how we find information online.
